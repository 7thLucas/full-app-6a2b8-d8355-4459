# Product Overview

## Identity
**Working Name**: EatNear (pending user confirmation)
**Category**: Location-based food discovery
**Platform**: Mobile-first web app

## Problem
Lower-to-mid income users — students, daily workers, families on tight budgets — waste 20+ minutes searching across multiple apps (Google Maps, delivery platforms, review sites) to find affordable food nearby. No existing tool puts budget-first discovery at its core. General market apps treat affordability as a filter, not an identity.

## Solution
A dedicated food finder built for the budget-conscious user. Open the app, see affordable food spots near your current location, sorted by price (lowest first) or by popularity (most trusted). Fast, focused, built for their economic reality — not a luxury app with a discount mode tacked on.

## Target Users
**Primary**: Lower-to-mid income individuals
- Students
- Daily workers
- Families on tight budgets

**Explicitly excluded**: High-income users, premium diners, the general mass market.

The product's identity is rooted in economic accessibility — users must feel the app was built *for* them, not adapted for them.

## Core Features (P0)
1. **Location-based discovery** — shows food spots near the user's current location
2. **Price sort** — ascending price order, lowest-cost options first
3. **Popularity sort** — most-rated / most-visited options first
4. **Budget filter** — user sets a maximum price ceiling

## Positioning
The food finder built *for* the budget-conscious — not *for everyone* with a budget mode. Economic accessibility is the product's identity, not a feature toggle. Community trust builds faster when users know it's made for them.

## Brand Tone
Honest. Practical. Community-forward. No luxury signaling. Direct and useful.

## Branding
- **Primary color**: Green (#16a34a) — savings, freshness, value
- **Accent color**: Orange (#ea580c) — warmth, food energy, community
- **Typography**: Bold, clean, accessible
